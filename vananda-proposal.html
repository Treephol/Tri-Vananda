Context
The world is shifting.

The shift
Luxury is now defined by how you live, not what you own.

The most trusted luxury groups are investing in philosophy, not product extension. Depth over breadth. Stewardship over expansion.

The opportunity
$5.6 trillion wellness economy. One white space unclaimed.

Daily, lived, integrated wellbeing — not a retreat, not a clinic — remains almost entirely without a credible luxury voice.

Montara's position
Trisara was never scaled. PRU never franchised. This is legacy behaviour.

02
Why This Brand. Why Now.
"Without a unifying wellness philosophy, each brand begins to tell its own story — and the whole becomes less than the sum of its parts."
The risk without Vananda

As the portfolio deepens — hospitality, residential, F&B, medical longevity — each brand will naturally develop its own wellness language. Guest understanding fragments. Brand equity disperses.

The strength with Vananda

A shared philosophy does not flatten the brands beneath it — it strengthens each one. When every touchpoint speaks from the same root belief, the group earns the coherence money cannot manufacture.

The right moment

Clinique La Prairie arrives Q4 2026. Vananda must exist before that moment — to provide the human and emotional dimension that science, by its nature, cannot. Together, they are complete.

07
The Name
Vananda
วานันดา · Sanskrit

Vana (वन) — Forest. The living world. Nature as teacher.

Ānanda (आनन्द) — Pure bliss. The deepest state of consciousness. Not pleasure, which is fleeting — but the stillness available when the body is at home in the world.

Together: the bliss found within nature. Joy that requires no performance.

Ownable
No competitive footprint in luxury wellness
Clean globally. Available for trademark across key markets. No inherited associations.
Pronounceable globally
Va · NAN · da
Three syllables. Intuitive. Works in English, Thai, French, Mandarin, Arabic. The phonetic rhythm is soft — unhurried, like the brand itself.
Enduring
No trend reference. No decade marker.
Like Aman. Like Como. A name that belongs to no specific era. It simply is.

11
Wordmark Study — Three Directions
The name must carry the brand's character before the guidelines are opened.

All three presented without colour — the mark must work in its rawest form.

Direction A
Recommended
Vananda
by Montara
Cormorant Garamond · Ultra Light Italic
The italic serif carries movement — something in flow, not fixed. At ultra-light weight it conveys the restraint and quiet confidence the brand holds. Classical proportions reference depth and heritage. The slight forward lean says the brand is always in gentle motion — living, never static.
Direction B
Alternative
Vananda
by Montara
Jost · Hairline · Wide Spaced Caps
Maximum restraint. The hairline weight communicates that Vananda does not need to announce itself. The extreme letter-spacing creates air — the same breathing space the brand offers to those who enter it. Pairs seamlessly with Montara's existing mark. Risk: can read as cold if not balanced by warm body typography.
Direction C
Alternative
VANANDA
by Montara
Cinzel · Light · Classical Roman
Roman inscription letterforms — the typography of permanence, of things carved to outlast their makers. Connects Vananda's Sanskrit roots to a typographic lineage of equal depth. Most naturally suited to environmental and architectural applications: wayfinding, stone, signage.
